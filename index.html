Cristian Bancayan

I am a Peruvian economist from the National University of San Marcos and hold a Master of Public Policy from the Harris School of Public Policy at the University of Chicago.

I have over six years of experience in the Peruvian public sector, specializing in monitoring, impact evaluation, and social innovation. Currently, I work as a Research Assistant at the Behavioral Insights and Parenting Lab at UChicago. Previously, I led the Ayni Lab Social at the Ministry of Development and Social Inclusion and worked at IDinsight in Lusaka, Zambia, evaluating the impact of a large-scale cash transfer program for households in Malawi.

I lead the Monitoring and Evaluation area at the Sinkumunchis Foundation and previously coordinated academic support for rural students at Yachay Wasi NGO. I am passionate about early childhood development, rural education, and leveraging public policy to expand opportunities for vulnerable communities. Among life's simple pleasures, I find joy in football — proudly supporting Universitario de Deportes — and immersing myself in the rhythms of cumbia and salsa music.
